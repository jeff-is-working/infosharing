# Executive Overview: TRL 2026 Budget & Layoff Controversy

*OSINT Landscape Brief — Draft v1 · 2026-04-17*

---

## The situation in one paragraph

Timberland Regional Library — the five-county Washington State district serving ~550,000 residents across Grays Harbor, Lewis, Mason, Pacific, and Thurston — entered an open governance and labor controversy in early 2026 when a $3.8M budgeted operating deficit prompted the Board of Trustees to authorize the layoff of 61 frontline staff (38% of the frontline workforce). After sustained public pressure, a State Auditor citizen complaint, a co-signed public letter from eight former trustees, the resignation of the Executive Director, and a union grievance settlement, **36 of the 44 involuntary layoffs were rescinded on 2026-04-15**. Eight probationary positions proceed on May 15; 17 voluntary departures proceed on April 30. The district is planning a November 2026 levy lid lift, its first serious levy attempt since **a 2009 failure at 54.86%–45.14%**.

## What OSINT synthesis surfaced that single-article coverage hasn't connected

1. **The deficit was knowingly adopted, not discovered.** On **2025-12-30**, in one special meeting, the Board unanimously (1) approved the 2026 Final Budget *with a $3,809,282.89 deficit on its face*, (2) set the 2026 levy increase at **$0.00** — forfeiting the ~$260K of growth the 1% WA cap permitted — and (3) renewed the Executive Director's 2-year contract with a 3% raise and a doubling of her personal-holiday allowance. The public framing that the crisis "came to light in late January" is inconsistent with this sequence. *Primary source: TRL minutes, motions 25-046, 25-048, 25-051.*

2. **The layoff reversal came through a grievance settlement, not Board goodwill.** Per AFSCME Local 3758B's own Reddit statement: *"In a 4/14/26 grievance settlement meeting, TRL agreed to rescind over 80% of the March 15th layoff notices."* TRL's subsequent 4/15 press statement emphasizing "this new direction" is a contractually-driven resolution, not magnanimity.

3. **Every Board action during the crisis has been unanimous.** The deficit budget (12/30), the zero levy increase (12/30), the ED comp renewal (12/30), the RIF authorization (2/25), the acceptance of the resignation (3/25) — all 5-0 or 4-0. Zero trustee dissent is on the public record. This aligns with the 2026-03-20 letter from **eight former trustees** — Bob Hall, Corby Varness, Brian Zylstra, Edna Fund, Brenda Hirschi, Gene Weaver, Judy Weaver, Nicolette Oliver — calling the current Board a "rubber stamp."

4. **The Board was explicitly advised on the only structural remedy in 2022 and chose not to act.** At the 2022-09 Board retreat, **Jill Jean, retired Director of Kitsap Regional Library**, walked the trustees through Kitsap's successful 2017 levy-lid-lift playbook. No levy lid lift was filed in 2022, 2023, 2024, or 2025. The November 2026 measure will be the district's first levy attempt in 17 years.

5. **The revenue architecture has no non-levy remedy at scale.** The 2026 budget is **93.7% dependent on property-tax levy**, 5.4% on timber, ~0.9% other. With the 1% WA annual cap and flat-levy adoption, operating costs outrun revenue every year. Absent a voter-approved lid lift, further cuts are mathematically unavoidable.

6. **Three parallel accountability tracks are active, not one.** The union/CBA track produced the 4/14 grievance settlement. **A citizen complaint has been filed with the Washington State Auditor's Office** (SAO portal shows at least two recent TRL reports, arn=1036255 and 1036267). The Board-governance track is producing new Financial Transparency and Planning Policy targeted for 2026-05-27 adoption, plus whistleblower/anti-retaliation policy review on 2026-05-12.

The operational takeaway: **this is not an incompetent board — it's a board that executed capital projects, knew the structural remedy, and chose not to act for three-plus years while adopting budgets that walked the district into a forced-cut posture.** That distinction matters for any remediation narrative.
